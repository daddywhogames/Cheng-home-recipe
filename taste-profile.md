# Taste Profile — Cheng household

_Built 2026-06-25 from the first batch of ~27 saved recipes. Will refine as more are added and as meal feedback comes in._

## Meal Structure (Refined)
Each meal (Lunch & Dinner) must have 3 distinct dishes:
1.  **Protein** (e.g., chicken, fish, beef, pork, tofu)
2.  **Vegetable** (Fiber-rich, e.g., spinach, bok choy, cauliflower, broccoli)
3.  **Carb** (e.g., rice, noodles, pasta, potatoes)

## Proteins (clear favorites)
- **Chicken** — the dominant protein. Drumsticks, wings, whole roast, legs. Both Western (paprika/garlic roasts, air-fryer "fried chicken") and Chinese (soy sauce wings, chicken+shiitake soup).
- **Fish/seafood** — strong second. Salmon (baked lemon-garlic, miso), cod (roasted, HK steamed), Teochew steamed fish, fish tacos.
- Beef/pork: minimal (only via japchae). Household leans chicken + fish.

## Cuisine leanings
- **Two clear poles:**
  1. **Western comfort** — roasts, baked/air-fried chicken, creamy mushroom pasta, herb potatoes/cauliflower.
  2. **East/Southeast Asian** — Chinese (soy sauce wings, steamed cod, spinach in superior broth, stir-fries, king oyster mushrooms), Teochew steamed fish, Korean japchae, Japanese miso salmon.
- Comfortable across both; not tied to one cuisine.

## Cooking methods preferred
- **Baking / roasting / air-frying** (low-fuss, oven-led) — very common.
- **Steaming** (fish especially — HK & Teochew style).
- **Stir-frying** for vegetables.
- Leans toward **practical, family-friendly, not fussy** techniques.

## Flavor notes
- Garlic, paprika, soy, ginger, lemon — recurring.
- Comfort-forward, mild-to-moderate spice (kid-friendly; 3 young children).
- Likes a mix of crispy (baked/air-fried) and clean/light (steamed fish, broths, salads).

## Vegetables / sides
- Fiber-rich vegetables: roasted cauliflower, Chinese spinach, everyday veg stir-fry, king oyster mushrooms, tomato-avocado salad, luffa, broccoli, bok choy.
- Potatoes are considered a carb/sidedish, not a vegetable (source of fiber).

## Practical constraints (from household.md)
- Cooking for 2 adults + 3 young children → kid-friendly, moderate spice, larger batches.
- Lunch + dinner, weekdays.
- Helper (Maritas) cooks; some meat dishes she buys fresh.

## "Surprise me" guidance (every 2 days)
When generating a surprise dish, stay inside the comfort zone but vary it:
- Rotate protein: chicken ↔ fish, occasional veg-forward main.
- Alternate Western roast/bake ↔ Asian steam/stir-fry.
- Keep it kid-friendly, achievable for a helper, ingredient-overlap with the staples above.
- Avoid: heavy spice, exotic/hard-to-source ingredients, pork/beef-heavy dishes unless requested.
